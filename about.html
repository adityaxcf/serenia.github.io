What is DAA?

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is a subject that teaches how to solve problems efficiently. The main focus is understanding how an algorithm behaves, how much time it takes, how much memory it uses and why one approach can be better than another.

In this course, we learn concepts like time and space complexity, recursion, divide and conquer, greedy methods, dynamic programming, hashing, searching, sorting and graph algorithms. These topics form the base for problem-solving in computer science.

Course Overview

The DAA theory course mainly focuses on analysing efficiencies and applying suitable techniques for different types of problems. We study how arrays, graphs, trees and different algorithms behave under various inputs.

By the end of the course, students should be able to:

  • Understand the purpose and working of algorithm strategies.
  • Compare efficiency of multiple algorithms.
  • Apply suitable techniques to solve real problems.
  • Work with graphs, trees and structured data models.

DAA Laboratory Overview

The lab part of the course helps us implement the algorithms we learn in theory. We write programs for searching, sorting, graph traversal, shortest paths and operations on trees like AVL or Red-Black Trees.

The course also includes a project. For my project, I designed a small smart city model named Serenia and related each part of the city with appropriate algorithms. This helped me connect classroom concepts to practical use.
