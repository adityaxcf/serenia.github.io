Algorithms Used in Serenia – A Smart City

Home About DAA Algorithms Project City Layout

1. Dijkstra’s Algorithm – Shortest Path Routing

Used for emergency vehicle routing, transport hub optimization, and daily navigation. Roads act as weighted graph edges, allowing the system to find the fastest route.

2. Breadth-First Search (BFS) – Water & Sewer Network

BFS helps trace connectivity in pipelines, detect blockages, and ensure even water distribution across Serenia.

3. Depth-First Search (DFS) – Security Surveillance

CCTV network monitoring uses DFS for deep scanning of blocks, tracking suspicious paths or exploring sub-areas fully.

4. Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) – Power & Streetlight Cost Optimization

MST algorithms (Prim’s/Kruskal’s) minimize cost when connecting power grids, wind turbines, and street lighting systems.

5. Greedy Algorithms – Waste Collection & Patrol Routes

Greedy methods help choose optimal local decisions such as shortest waste collection routes and police patrol scheduling.

6. Divide & Conquer – City Zoning & Traffic Segmentation

Serenia is divided into functional zones for planning and traffic distribution. Large problems are split into smaller subproblems.

7. Dynamic Programming – Hospital Resource Management

DP optimizes medical supply distribution, bed allocation, and emergency scheduling during high demand.

8. Sorting Algorithms – Market & Government Records

Sorting helps manage databases like tax records, property data, and market vendor management.

9. Hashing – Identity, Vehicles & Security Systems

Hashing allows quick lookup for citizen records, vehicle numbers, and face-recognition systems.
